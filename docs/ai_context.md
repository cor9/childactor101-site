# AI Context

This document provides essential context for AI assistants contributing to the Child Actor 101 Platform.

Read this document before making architectural, design, UX, SEO, branding, or content decisions.

---

# Project Summary

Child Actor 101 is the largest project in an educational ecosystem built by Corey Ralston.

Although it began as a Facebook community for parents of young actors, it has evolved into a comprehensive educational platform consisting of websites, software tools, courses, podcasts, newsletters, directories, and industry resources.

The goal of this repository is to unify those experiences under a cohesive visual identity and information architecture while preserving existing SEO and customer trust.

---

# About Corey Ralston

Corey Ralston is:

- Youth Talent Manager
- Acting Coach
- Former Child Actor
- Writer
- Educator
- Entrepreneur
- Founder of Child Actor 101

Corey's primary expertise is helping parents understand the television and film industry while helping young actors build long-term careers through training, preparation, professionalism, and realistic expectations.

Corey is not positioned as a celebrity coach.

He is positioned as a trusted educator and industry guide.

Every page should reinforce that trust.

# Mission

Child Actor 101 exists to help families confidently navigate the television and film industry.

Everything we build should reduce confusion.

Not increase it.

Parents should leave feeling:

"I understand this better."

# Educational Philosophy

Child Actor 101 teaches before selling.

---

# Illustration Philosophy

Illustrations are one of the strongest parts of the Child Actor 101 brand.

Every article should feature a custom Pixar-inspired cinematic illustration.

Illustrations are not decoration.

They communicate the emotional theme of the lesson before the reader begins reading.

Illustrations should feel:

Warm

Expressive

Hopeful

Educational

Story-driven

Cinematic

Avoid generic AI imagery.

Avoid clip art.

Avoid corporate illustrations.

---

# Writing Style

Write as an experienced educator.

Not as a marketer.

Explain complicated concepts simply.

Assume parents are intelligent but unfamiliar with the entertainment industry.

Avoid unnecessary jargon.

Avoid fear-based language.

Avoid exaggerated promises.

Avoid clickbait.

Prefer:

Clear explanations

Examples

Stories

Practical advice

Honest expectations

# SEO Philosophy

SEO exists to help parents find trustworthy information.

Never create content solely to manipulate search rankings.

Every article should satisfy real user intent.

Every page should have:

Metadata

Structured data

Internal links

Related resources

Author information

The Corey Ralston authority page serves as the canonical author profile.

---

# Information Architecture

The ecosystem is organized around user needs.

Not products.

Navigation should help parents answer:

What should I learn?

How do I prepare?

What tool do I need?

What should I do next?

# Decision-Making Principles

When multiple design options exist, choose the one that:

Builds trust

Improves clarity

Educates parents

Simplifies navigation

Strengthens the ecosystem

Improves accessibility

Supports long-term scalability

Avoid unnecessary complexity.

---

# Things Never To Do

Do not use fear to sell.

Do not create false urgency.

Do not imitate generic coaching websites.

Do not overload pages with competing calls to action.

Do not prioritize conversions over trust.

Do not introduce visual clutter.

Do not break consistency with the established Child Actor 101 identity.

---

# Definition of Success

Success is not measured by how many products are shown.

Success is measured by whether a first-time parent leaves saying:

"I finally understand what to do next."

Every contribution should move the platform closer to becoming the most trusted educational resource for families navigating professional youth acting.
