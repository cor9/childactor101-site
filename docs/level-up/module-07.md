## Source Material Summary

This module is framed as a wellness and resilience guide for parents of child actors, with a strong emphasis on self-esteem, emotional health, and healthy boundaries.

### Chapter 1: Understanding the Landscape

Core themes:

- child actors live in both a professional world and a childhood world
- parents must help balance education, acting, play, and rest
- pressure to perform can distort a child's self-image if left unmanaged

Key ideas:

- protect normalcy
- prioritize education
- understand the emotional cost of constant evaluation
- build a strong support system

### Chapter 2: Fostering a Positive Self-Image

Core themes:

- praise effort and resilience, not just bookings or performance
- affirm character traits beyond acting
- encourage self-discovery outside the industry
- introduce healthy role models

This section strongly supports future lessons about identity, confidence, and helping kids feel valuable beyond the work.

### Chapter 3: Navigating Rejection and Setbacks

Core themes:

- rejection is inevitable
- rejection should not be treated as a verdict on worth
- resilience can be taught

Key strategies:

- let kids express disappointment
- help them reframe rejection constructively
- keep other areas of life active and meaningful
- separate actor identity from personal identity

### Chapter 4: Healthy Boundaries in the Industry

Core themes:

- professional boundaries
- personal boundaries
- online boundaries
- open communication
- family time and self-care

Key ideas:

- children need permission to say no
- parents should create a safe space for honest conversation
- rest, play, and normal family life are not optional extras

### Chapter 5: The Role of Parents and Guardians

Core themes:

- being an advocate
- balancing support with independence
- managing parental emotion and expectation

This section supports future lessons about parental mindset, pressure management, and how to support a child actor without making the career emotionally unsafe.
